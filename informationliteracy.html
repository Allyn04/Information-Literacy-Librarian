Information Literacy

Helping you search smarter, think critically, and use information wisely.

What is Information Literacy?

Information literacy is the ability to find, evaluate, and use information effectively. It is not just about searching on Google, but about knowing where to look, how to judge what’s real or fake, and how to use information ethically and responsibly. In today’s digital world, where so much information is available online, information literacy helps us avoid fake news, plagiarism, and misinformation.

It involves skills like identifying a research need, choosing the right sources (books, articles, databases), checking credibility, citing correctly, and thinking critically. This skill is very important for students, teachers, researchers, and even parents.

My Role as an Information Literacy Librarian

As a future Information Literacy Librarian, my main job is to guide users — especially students — in becoming smart, confident, and responsible users of information. I help others learn how to:

  • Search for trustworthy and reliable sources online or in the library
  • Understand how to avoid plagiarism by citing sources properly
  • Recognize fake or biased information on the internet
  • Use online databases, library catalogues, and academic resources
  • Apply critical thinking when using digital content

I don’t just give answers — I teach people how to find their own answers, and that’s a powerful skill for life.

My Services

  • One-on-One Support: Helping individuals who struggle to find the right sources for assignments
  • Workshops: Conducting short sessions on citation tools, research strategies, or using online databases
  • Guided Searching: Teaching the best ways to search for information on Google Scholar, online journals, or the library system
  • Digital Safety: Guiding users on how to stay safe online and avoid untrustworthy content

Why It Matters: Benefits for Users

  • Improved academic performance through correct and strong sources
  • Save time and effort when doing school or university work
  • Gain confidence in using digital tools and research platforms
  • Learn how to avoid mistakes like plagiarism or relying on fake news
  • Feel more in control when searching for information

How This Website Supports Information Literacy

This website was designed not just to introduce who I am, but also to serve as a digital space where users can learn more about information literacy. It includes simple navigation, clear explanations, and a friendly interface — so that even beginners can benefit from it.

I believe librarians today are not just working with books, but are part of the digital journey of every learner. That’s why I created this platform — to support, guide, and connect with those who want to become better information users.
